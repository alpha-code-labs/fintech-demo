TRADED VOLUME vs DELIVERY VOLUME — A Story
=============================================

THE FISH MARKET ANALOGY
------------------------

Imagine you're standing outside Crawford Market in Mumbai watching the fish
section. You want to figure out which fish is suddenly in high demand today.

You see 1,000 kg of pomfret changing hands throughout the day. That's TRADED
VOLUME — the total amount that was bought and sold.

But here's the catch — some of those traders are just middlemen. They buy
pomfret at 8 AM, sell it at 10 AM to another middleman, who sells it at
12 PM to a restaurant. That same 10 kg of fish got "traded" 3 times. The
traded volume says 30 kg, but only 10 kg actually left the market.

DELIVERY VOLUME is the fish that actually went home with someone. The buyer
said "I want this, I'm keeping it." They walked out of the market with it.

IN STOCK MARKET TERMS
----------------------

On NSE/BSE, when you buy a stock, you can either:

(a) TAKE DELIVERY — the shares actually move into your demat account.
    You own them. You're an investor. You believe in this stock enough
    to hold it.

(b) TRADE INTRADAY — you buy at 10 AM, sell at 2 PM. The shares never
    enter your demat account. You're a speculator. You just want to
    profit from today's price movement.

TRADED VOLUME = all shares that changed hands (both types combined)
DELIVERY VOLUME = only the shares that actually moved to someone's
                  demat account (type a)

EXAMPLE: TWO STOCKS ON THE SAME DAY
-------------------------------------

Stock A: Tata Motors
- Traded volume: 50 lakh shares
- Delivery volume: 5 lakh shares (10% delivery)
- Price up 3%

What happened: Mostly intraday traders playing the stock. Buying and
selling within the day. Only 10% actually wanted to OWN the stock.
The 3% move is unreliable — it could reverse tomorrow because there's
no real conviction behind it. Just noise.

Stock B: Some mid-cap chemical company
- Traded volume: 20 lakh shares (normally trades 2 lakh/day)
- Delivery volume: 14 lakh shares (70% delivery)
- Price up 8%

What happened: Volume is 10x normal. AND 70% of buyers actually took
delivery — they want to OWN this stock. Someone with serious money
is accumulating. They know something. This is a SIGNAL.

THIS IS WHAT GORDON LOOKS FOR.

WHY DELIVERY VOLUME MATTERS MORE
----------------------------------

Think about it like real estate.

If 100 people visit a flat in Gurgaon on a Sunday — that's "traded volume."
Lots of activity. Looks hot.

But if only 2 people put down a token — that's "delivery volume."
The 98 others were just window shopping.

Now imagine another flat in a random part of Noida. Normally gets 5 visitors
a week. Suddenly 40 people show up and 30 put down a token. THAT is the
signal. Something changed. Maybe a metro line got approved. Maybe a big
company is setting up office nearby. You don't know WHY yet, but the
delivery data tells you something real is happening.

GORDON'S SPECIFIC FILTER
--------------------------

He scans for stocks where:
1. Weekly traded volume is 5x the annual average
   (something unusual is happening)
2. Price has jumped 5-10% alongside this volume
   (the move is significant, not just noise)

He uses WEEKLY charts because:
- Daily is too noisy — one day of high volume could mean anything
- Monthly is too slow — by the time monthly data confirms, the move
  is already well underway
- Weekly is the sweet spot — filters out daily noise but catches
  trends early enough

And within that volume, DELIVERY volume is what he cares about most.
Because that tells you: are people actually putting their money where
their mouth is?

A stock spiking on high traded volume but low delivery = traders playing
games, will reverse.

A stock spiking on high traded volume AND high delivery = someone is
accumulating with conviction. Pay attention.

WHERE TO FIND THIS DATA
-------------------------
- NSE website publishes delivery volume data daily
- Screener.in shows volume data
- TradingView shows traded volume on charts
- Some brokers (Zerodha, etc.) show delivery percentage in their tools

THIS IS THE FIRST STEP IN GORDON'S SYSTEM
-------------------------------------------
Everything else — breakout patterns, fundamental analysis, sector
rotation — comes AFTER this filter. If a stock doesn't pass the
volume + price test, Gordon doesn't even look at it. No matter
how good the company is.

That's what he means by "the pool from which we will fish."
No volume spike = not in the pool = doesn't exist for him.
